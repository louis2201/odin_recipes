Pork Adobo

Pork Adobo is a beloved Filipino dish known for its rich, savory, and slightly tangy flavor. This recipe features tender pork simmered in a fragrant blend of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and black pepper, creating a deep umami taste with a hint of zest. As it slowly cooks, the meat absorbs the bold flavors, resulting in a dish that is both hearty and comforting. Best served with steamed rice, this classic favorite embodies the essence of Filipino home cooking—simple, flavorful, and satisfying.

  • 2 lbs pork belly
  • 2 tablespoons garlic, crushed
    • 5 dried bay leaves
  • 4 tablespoons vinegar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon peppercorn
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  1. Marinate the Pork: In a bowl, combine the pork belly, soy sauce, and crushed garlic. Mix well, ensuring each piece is coated. Cover and marinate for at least 1 hour to let the flavors penetrate the meat.
  2. Sauté the Marinated Pork: Heat a pot over medium heat. Add the marinated pork belly, including the marinade and garlic. Cook for a few minutes until the pork starts to brown.
  3. Add Seasonings and Simmer: Pour in water, whole peppercorns, and dried bay leaves. Stir the mixture and bring it to a boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for 40 minutes to 1 hour, or until the pork becomes tender. If the liquid reduces too quickly, add more water as needed.
  4. Incorporate Vinegar: Pour the vinegar into the pot. Allow the mixture to re-boil without stirring. Let it simmer for an additional 12 to 15 minutes to meld the flavors together.
  5. Season to Taste: Taste the sauce and add salt if necessary, adjusting to your preference.
  6. Serve: Once the pork is tender and the sauce has reached your desired consistency, transfer the Pork Adobo to a serving dish. Serve hot, ideally with steamed rice.
