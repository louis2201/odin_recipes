Beef Kaldereta

Beef Kaldereta is a rich and hearty Filipino stew known for its tender beef chunks slow-cooked in a flavorful tomato-based sauce. Infused with garlic, onions, and bell peppers, this dish gets its depth from liver spread, which adds a creamy, savory richness. Carrots, potatoes, and olives enhance its texture and taste, while a touch of chili gives it a mild kick. Often enjoyed during gatherings, this comforting dish pairs perfectly with steamed rice, making it a beloved staple in Filipino cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef chuck, sliced into cubes
  • 2 medium carrots, sliced
  • 1 large potato, cubed
  • 1 small green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 small red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 Knorr Beef Cube
  • 1 can (8 oz) tomato sauce
  • 5 tablespoons liver spread
  • 3 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 medium yellow onion, chopped
  • ¼ teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • Salt and ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • 6 tablespoons cooking oil

Cooking Steps

  1. Sauté Aromatics: Heat cooking oil in a pot over medium heat. Add chopped onions and crushed garlic; sauté until the onions become soft.
  2. Brown the Beef: Introduce the cubed beef into the pot. Cook for approximately 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the beef's exterior turns light brown.
  3. Incorporate Seasonings: Add dried bay leaves and chili flakes or crushed pepper to the pot. Stir the mixture to evenly distribute the spices.
  4. Add Liver Spread: Stir in the liver spread, ensuring it blends well with the beef and aromatics.
  5. Pour in Liquids: Pour the tomato sauce and beef broth into the pot. Stir to combine all ingredients.
  6. Simmer Until Tender: Bring the mixture to a boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer until the beef becomes tender. This typically takes about 30 minutes when using a pressure cooker or 1 to 2 hours with a regular pot.
  7. Add Vegetables: Once the beef is tender, add the sliced potatoes and carrots.
